Craig Waitt, Ph. D.

Assistant Professor of Chemistry, Trine University

I'm Craig Waitt, Assistant Professor of Chemistry at Trine University, and I'm excited to welcome you to my website. As a researcher and educator in the field of chemistry, I'm passionate about using computational tools to help us better understand the intricacies of molecular behavior.

My research focuses on understanding the stability and reactivity of molecules and catalysts, and I regularly employ Density Functional Theory (DFT), Wavefunction Theory (WFT), and Machine Learning (ML) tools. But what really gets me excited is sharing this knowledge with students who are eager to learn how computers can be used to accelerate their own research aims.

As an educator, I believe that hands-on experience is key to learning and growth. That's why I'm always looking for opportunities to collaborate with undergraduate students who share my enthusiasm for computational chemistry. Whether you're a student looking to gain some experience with computational methods or simply someone who wants to learn more about how computers can be used in scientific research, I'd love to chat with you.

On this website, you'll find information about my research group, current and previous projects, tools, and opportunities for undergraduate students to get involved. I hope you'll take a look around, and maybe even drop by and chat if something catches your eye. Let's explore the fascinating world of computational chemistry together!

Before moving to Trine, I have had the privilege of working under the esteemed guidance of Professor William Schneider at the university of Notre Dame, where we utilize computational tools to elucidating chemistries occuring on catalysts at the atomistic level. My primary research thrust revolves around understanding the intricacies and nuances of hydrothermal stability on zeolite materials. My work seeks to unravel the behavior of molecules and materials at the atomic level, utlizing first-principles technqiues.

Beyond my research endeavors, my passion for education and pedagogy takes center stage. Firmly believing in the transformative power of education, I am dedicated to assisting students across diverse backgrounds in realizing their full potential. Having imparted knowledge in various undergraduate chemistry courses, I take pride in the positive feedback received for my adeptness in elucidating complex concepts and rendering chemistry both engaging and accessible.

My commitment to education has not gone unnoticed, with accolades such as teaching awards from the University of Notre Dame underlining my effectiveness in the classroom. As a mentor, I find immense joy in guiding students to develop the skills and confidence necessary for success in academic and professional spheres. Constantly seeking avenues for enhancing my teaching practice and staying abreast of the latest educational developments, I aspire to make a lasting impact on the next generation of scientists and educators through a harmonious blend of teaching and research.

Thank you for visiting my website and feel free to reach out if you have any queastions!
