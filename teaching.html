Teaching

My philosophy of education is that all students have unique perspectives and must have a stimulating educational environment where they can grow mentally, emotionally, and socially. It is my responsibility as a teacher to create an inclusive environment that promotes not only the learning of the content-matter related to the course, but also critical thinking skills that students can apply in other aspects of their lives.

Education is a process of teaching to your students, and learning from your students. This is a life-long process of learning new strategies, ideas, and philosophies. I believe in order to be a successful teacher, you need to be able to navigate around this delicate balance of instructing and being instructed.

This one-semester course, offered in the Fall, provides a thorough grounding in the fundamental principles governing chemical structure and reactivity.
